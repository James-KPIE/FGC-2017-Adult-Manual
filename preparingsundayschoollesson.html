PREPARING A SUNDAY SCHOOL LESSON

1. Start lesson preparation early in the week, so that your reading and experiences during the week may be incorporated in your teaching.

2. Have a central aim in your lesson.

3. Read the background scripture passages from several versions for clarification.

4. Develop a clear outline for the class to follow. Choose from your teacher's manual the points you plan to emphasize.

5. Arrange time for: prayer, review, Bible reading, discussion, pupil's book, memory work, application and closing. But remember, BE FLEXIBLE!

6. Remember the needs of individuals in your class (discover these through visitation in their home). Keep in mind that you are teaching persons and not just a lesson. Know the age level characteristics of the group you are teaching.

7. Develop lesson note for each topic, add illustrations, outlines and other teaching aids each year as you continue to teach.

8. Know the work required in the pupil's workbook. Determine how to use it in class review, workbook and discussion.

9. Plan an interesting approach to capture students' attention. This may be a question to stimulate thought, a problem to solve, a true – to- life story to illustrate the lesson, or a visual aid to motivate interest in the lesson.

10. Plan a variety of methods, visual, object lesson, drama, stories, sword drills, questions and answers, discussion, writing, buzz group, or problem solving.

11. Plan thoroughly for class participation.

12. Develop study questions to cause the pupils to search the Bible for personal investigations. Ask them: who, what, when, where, why? (For example: Ask, “What does the Bible say? Or “What does it mean? etc).

13. Allow pupils to ask questions. If you don't know the answer, admit it and state that you will seek the answer during the week, or assign the question to the class for further study.

14. Prepare the room before the class period which includes: arranging chair, visual aid equipment, decorations, and objects.

15. Pray for wisdom in preparation, clarity in presentation, and sincerity in application.
